Framework to Rule Them All

The CRAFT Framework

Write prompts that actually work. Every single time.

C

Context

Give background info. What's the situation? Who's the audience? What do you already have?

R

Role

Tell the AI who to be. "You are a senior product manager at a SaaS startup" beats "help me."

A

Action

State exactly what you need done. Be specific: "Write 5 subject lines" not "help with email."

F

Format

Define the output structure. Bullet points? Table? JSON? Numbered list? 280 characters?

T

Tone

Set the voice. Professional, casual, witty, academic, empathetic. Match your audience.

Example using CRAFT
[Context] I run a DTC skincare brand targeting women 25-40. We're launching a vitamin C serum at $38.
[Role] You are a conversion copywriter who has written for Glossier and The Ordinary.
[Action] Write 5 email subject lines for the launch announcement that drive opens.
[Format] Numbered list. Each under 50 characters. Include one emoji option.
[Tone] Confident, clean, slightly playful. Never salesy or desperate.

Competitive Moat Analysis
You are a strategy consultant from Bain & Company. I run [describe your business, revenue, and market]. Analyze my competitive position using Porter's Five Forces. For each force, rate the threat level (Low/Medium/High), explain why, and give one specific action I can take this quarter to strengthen my moat. Format as a table with columns: Force | Threat Level | Analysis | Recommended Action.

Give me a specific recommended price point with reasoning, and design a 3-tier pricing structure (Good/Better/Best) with feature breakdowns for each tier.

LinkedIn Post (Viral Format)
You are a LinkedIn ghostwriter for Fortune 500 executives. Write a LinkedIn post about [topic/lesson] using the hook-story-insight format. Requirements: First line must be a pattern-interrupt hook that stops the scroll (no "I'm excited to announce"). Include a personal or relatable story in 3-4 short paragraphs. End with a non-obvious takeaway. Use line breaks after every 1-2 sentences. Add a conversation-starting question at the end. Total length: 150-200 words. Do NOT use hashtags or emojis in the body. Tone: thoughtful and human, not performative.
Newsletter Issue Writer

Structure it as: 1) One-liner TLDR at the top, 2) The big story (200 words, opinionated take, not a summary), 3) "What this means for you" (3 actionable bullet points), 4) Quick hits section (4 interesting links with one-sentence commentary each), 5) One thought-provoking closing question. Twitter/X Thread Builder
You are a Twitter ghostwriter whose threads routinely get 1M+ impressions. Write a 10-tweet thread about [topic]. Total under 150 words. Tone: premium but approachable, like Allbirds or Aesop.
Code Review & Refactor
You are a principal software engineer at Google who specializes in clean architecture. Review this code: [paste code]. Analyze it for: 1) Bugs or potential runtime errors, 2) Performance issues (time/space complexity), 3) Security vulnerabilities, 4) Readability and naming conventions, 5) SOLID principle violations. For each issue found, rate its severity (Critical/Warning/Suggestion), explain the problem, and provide the refactored code. At the end, give an overall code quality score out of 10 with your top 3 priorities to fix.

Debug & Fix Error
You are a senior debugging specialist. I'm getting this error: [paste error message and stack trace]. Here's the relevant code: [paste code]. My environment: [language version, framework, OS]. Don't just explain the error — give me: 1) Root cause in one sentence, 2) The exact fix with corrected code, 3) Why this fix works, 4) How to prevent this class of error in the future (e.g., a linting rule, type annotation, or test), 5) Any other potential issues you spotted in the surrounding code.

Test Suite Generator

Resume Bullet Point Rewriter

Rewrite each bullet using the XYZ formula: "Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]." Interview Answer Builder (STAR Method)
You are an executive interview coach. I'm interviewing for [role] at [company]. The question is: "[paste behavioral interview question]." My rough experience: [paste a few sentences about what you did]. Structure my answer using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Make the Situation concise (2 sentences), the Action section the longest and most detailed (show leadership and decision-making), and the Result quantified with a business outcome. Keep total answer under 90 seconds when spoken aloud. End with a forward-looking statement connecting this experience to the role.

Feynman Technique Explainer
You are a world-class teacher who can explain anything to anyone. I want to deeply understand [complex topic]. Explain it to me using the Feynman Technique: 1) Explain it like I'm a smart 12-year-old (no jargon, use analogies from everyday life), 2) Identify the 3 concepts where most people get confused and clarify each one, 3) Create a simple mental model or visual metaphor I can use to remember the core idea, 4) Give me 3 "test yourself" questions that prove whether I truly understand it or am just memorizing. If I can't answer those questions, explain what I'm missing.

I can dedicate [hours per week]. My learning style preference: [visual/reading/hands-on/video]. Socratic Tutor
You are a Socratic tutor. Your job is to help me learn [subject/topic] by asking me questions, never by giving me answers directly. Start with a foundational question to gauge my current understanding. Based on my answer: if I'm right, go deeper and ask a harder question. If I'm wrong, don't correct me — instead, ask a simpler question that guides me to discover my mistake myself. After every 3 exchanges, give me a brief "checkpoint" summary of what I've demonstrated I understand. Adapt your difficulty in real-time. Begin now.
Flashcard Set Generator
You are a spaced-repetition and memory science expert. I need to memorize key concepts from [subject/chapter/topic]. Here are my notes: [paste notes or describe what you need to learn]. Create 20 optimized flashcards. Rules: front side should be a specific question (not "define X" but "What happens when X meets Y?"), back side should be concise (under 30 words), include 3 "reversible" cards (can be quizzed in either direction), add 2 "application" cards that test real-world use, not just recall. Tag each card with difficulty: Basic/Intermediate/Advanced. Format as a numbered table: # | Front | Back | Difficulty.
